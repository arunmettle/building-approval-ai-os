# MVP Wedge

## Recommended Wedge

The blueprint is intentionally global. The production entry point should not be.

This repository adopts the following initial wedge:

- Geography: Queensland, Australia
- Authorities: 3 to 5 councils with relatively accessible planning/building information
- Permit classes: decks, pergolas, sheds, patios
- Buyer: installers first
- User workflow: pre-quote pre-check and submission-readiness

## Why This Wedge

- Installer workflows have clearer ROI than homeowner self-serve.
- Small residential accessory structures have repetitive decision patterns.
- Submission-readiness is lower risk than automated regulatory conclusions.
- A narrow jurisdiction set makes evidence quality and rule versioning tractable.

## First User Promise

Before quoting or submitting, an installer can determine:

- likely approval pathway
- likely required documents
- visible risk factors
- missing information that blocks confident assessment
- where professional review is recommended

## Product Language Guardrails

Allowed phrasing:

- likely approval pathway
- evidence-supported risk rating
- likely required documents
- potential compliance issues
- professional review recommended

Disallowed phrasing:

- approved
- legally compliant
- guaranteed approval

## Exit Criteria For This Wedge

- At least 20 installer workflow interviews
- At least 3 pilot customers
- At least 100 assessed jobs with reviewer feedback
- Citation coverage above 95 percent for user-facing claims
- Unsupported claim rate below 1 percent in reviewed outputs
