# Queensland Pilot Jurisdictions

Accessed: 2026-06-13

## Selected Pilot Councils

The first three pilot councils are:

1. Brisbane City Council
2. City of Moreton Bay
3. Sunshine Coast Council

## Selection Rationale

These three councils were selected because they each show a workable combination of:

- public planning and building guidance for the target permit classes
- property and planning lookup tooling
- development application or approval search capability
- some level of planning or spatial data accessibility
- visible pathways for building and planning escalation

Gold Coast remains a strong backup candidate, but the first three above provide a cleaner starting set for the wedge.

## Evidence Snapshot

### Brisbane City Council

- Deck guidance is specific enough for deterministic starter rules, including common thresholds around area, height, roofing, side length, structural impact, and pool enclosure impact.
- City Plan Online supports address, lot plan, street, and suburb search with zones and overlays.
- Development.i exposes past and current application details and basic property information.
- Brisbane also publishes City Plan 2014 zoning data through its open data portal.

Primary sources:

- <https://www.brisbane.qld.gov.au/building-and-planning/getting-started-on-your-project/residential-projects/deck>
- <https://www.brisbane.qld.gov.au/building-and-planning/supporting-documents-and-online-tools/city-plan-online>
- <https://developmenti.brisbane.qld.gov.au/>
- <https://data.brisbane.qld.gov.au/explore/dataset/cp14-zoning-overlay/>

### City of Moreton Bay

- Building permits guidance explicitly covers structures such as carports and patios and routes users to private certifiers.
- My Property Look Up exposes zones, accepted building heights, and environmental issues for a property.
- The planning scheme has both online access and interactive/PDF maps.
- The council also publishes a DataHub for location-based open data.

Primary sources:

- <https://www.moretonbay.qld.gov.au/Services/Building-Development/Building/Building-Permits-And-Approval>
- <https://www.moretonbay.qld.gov.au/Services/Building-Development/Planning-Schemes/My-Property-Look-Up>
- <https://www.moretonbay.qld.gov.au/Services/Building-Development/Planning-Schemes/MBRC>
- <https://www.moretonbay.qld.gov.au/Services/Building-Development/Planning-Schemes/MBRC/Maps>
- <https://datahub.moretonbay.qld.gov.au/>

### Sunshine Coast Council

- Building approval guidance is unusually explicit for the target class, including threshold language for decks, pergolas, verandahs, patios and balconies.
- Interactive mapping points users to Development.i site reports and MyMaps zoning lookup.
- The planning scheme and open data hub expose mapping and overlay resources.
- The council provides building search access for records retrieval workflows.

Primary sources:

- <https://www.sunshinecoast.qld.gov.au/development/building/what-needs-building-approval>
- <https://www.sunshinecoast.qld.gov.au/development/planning-documents/sunshine-coast-planning-scheme-2014/interactive-mapping>
- <https://www.sunshinecoast.qld.gov.au/development/searches/building-records-search>
- <https://data.sunshinecoast.qld.gov.au/search?tags=qld>

## Backup Candidate

### City of Gold Coast

Gold Coast has viable official material, including domestic building works guidance, setback guidance, mapping search, and building approvals search. It remains a likely fourth jurisdiction once the first three are profiled.

Primary sources:

- <https://www.goldcoast.qld.gov.au/Planning-building/Building-applications/Domestic-building-works>
- <https://www.goldcoast.qld.gov.au/Planning-building/Building-applications/Domestic-building-works/Do-your-building-works-need-approval>
- <https://www.goldcoast.qld.gov.au/Planning-building/Building-applications/Setbacks-for-buildings-or-structures>
- <https://www.goldcoast.qld.gov.au/Planning-building/PD-Online>
- <https://www.goldcoast.qld.gov.au/Planning-building/Buying-and-selling-property/Building-approvals-search>

## State-Level Baseline Sources

These state sources should be modeled once and reused across all Queensland councils:

- accepted development under the Building Regulation 2021
- DA forms and supporting guidance
- DA Rules and referral guidance

## Decision

Proceed with Brisbane, Moreton Bay, and Sunshine Coast as the first ingestion and assessment pilots.
